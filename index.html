Biography

I’m a postdoc with Mathias Payer’s HexHive group at EPFL IC. I’m interested in various aspects of cyber-physical and wireless systems security.

I hold a PhD in Computer Science from the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD). During my PhD I’ve researched ways to design, implement and evaluate secure cyber-physical and wireless systems. As part of my PhD I spent six months at the Computer Science Department of the University of Oxford (UK) as a visiting researcher advised by Kasper Rasmussen, and ten months as a visiting researcher at the Helmholtz Center for Information Security (CISPA) advised by Nils Ole Tippenhauer.

I hold a BS and MS in Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering from the University of Bologna (UniBO). I spent three months at the University of Massachusetts (UMass Amherst) as a research assistant to prepare my master thesis about random number generation and testing advised by Wayne Burleson and Riccardo Rovatti.
